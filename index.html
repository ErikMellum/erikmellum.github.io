Mio/Zoom Internal FAQ

This FAQ is for internal use ONLY. Last updated on Jun 3, 2022.

Basics

Q: What is Mio Interoperability?

Mio makes it possible for users to send messages across Zoom, Microsoft Teams, Slack, and Webex so that everyone can chat with each other from their preferred platform.

Q: What are the problems Mio is solving?

Our customers regularly express their frustration at the number of communication platforms that are used in parallel by their organization. A combination of apps – like Microsoft Teams, Slack, Cisco Webex – is a common setup in most enterprises. Having to switch between different platforms creates an artificial barrier between teams, costing them time and fractured communication. Users are more productive when they use a familiar tool. Interoperability solutions will allow users to keep using their preferred chat app. Two top use cases Mio is solving:

  1. Intra-company communication - communication between users working at the same company but on different platforms
  2. Migration to Zoom Chat - customers who want to migrate from Slack/Teams to Zoom. To minimize migration resistance, companies want to give their employees time to transition. And during that time, they need a short-term interop solution like Mio 

*Interoperability with external contacts such as customers, vendors and contractors is not supported.

GA is currently scheduled for June 2022.

In the initial phase, interoperability will be limited to internal only, as in communication between users working at the same company but on different platforms.
  • Threading and reaction to bot messages won’t be available in the initial launch but will be in the immediate follow-up release in a month. Messages and emoji reactions will appear, just not visually organized around specific messages. Bots are only used users messages from other platforms do not have a Zoom identity. Q: What are the technical requirements to use Mio on the Zoom side?

  1. Purchased Mio licenses
  2. The latest Mio app is installed in the account by their account admin
  3. The latest Zoom app, 5.11.1 or higher. Users should re-login after installing the latest Zoom app. If users began using Mio without updating their client, they will encounter UI and local data storage issues which will persist even if they upgrade later.
  4. Advanced Chat Encryption is disabled in the account
  5. Account is Zoom Cloud-enabled

Q: Can Zoomies get access to Mio internally for day-to-day work (not testing)?

We do not have plans to support using Mio internally for day-to-day work communication in the foreseeable future:

  1. Zoomies are required to use Zoom Chat, not other chat tools. We do not want to encourage the usage of other platforms.
  2. Mio is a third-party tool. In order to install Mio for Zoom employees, it has to go through Vendor Selection which comprise of compliance, security, and privacy assessments. If approved, then negotiate and sign vendor agreement with Mio.

Almost instantly.

On Zoom we use ChatBot, and Full account sync. Full account sync which is where the user accounts exist on Zoom but users primarily use another platform to chat and authorize Mio to act on behalf of the actual platform user. Here’s a walkthrough of the MioHub where Admin will add platforms and conduct channel and user sync.

Q: How are channels on different platforms linked?

There are two ways to link channels

  1. Admins can log into Mio Hub to link channels in Zoom to channels in Slack, Teams, and WebEx. Admin can only link public channels.
  2. Users can add Mio bots to the channels they want to turn into Universal channels and corresponding channels will be created on the other platforms. Users can use this method to link both public and private channels.

Q: Can Zoom, Teams, and Slack users chat together in one channel?

It is only possible to connect a channel from two platforms - Zoom<>Teams, Zoom<>Slack, Zoom<>WebEx. This is more a UI limitation as opposed to the underlying tech. We will prioritize to support multiple platforms based on feedback from customers.

File Transfer & Storage

Q: How are files uploaded and transferred between different platforms?

When a file is sent from a client such as MS Teams, Mio gets an endpoint from the client source then generates a URL. This public URL is sent to the target platform’s client. A user clicks on the link and is redirected via a browser to perform authentication. Once authenticated and additional checks have been performed (i.e platform login, the user is in the channel), Mio connects up to the source platform and streams the contents to the browser window.

Occasionally, a real-time stream between the platforms is not possible. In this scenario, the file is requested and temporarily cached on the Mio server before the outbound connection is made.

Q: How long does Mio store uploaded files for?

Mio never stores any kind of attachments on its servers. All transactions are performed in memory and streamed from the source platform in real-time, where Mio acts as a proxy between the platforms. File retention on Zoom and other platforms will follow customers’ data configurations on the platforms.

Q: What if the customer doesn’t allow file transfer?

Mio can disable file sharing in Mio Hub (account dashboard on Mio).

Data

Q: Do you use ”persistent storage” for this exchange? What do you store? How long is the message retained?

Mio never saves any messages. Messages are translated in memory. Only metadata is retained for future reconciliation.

Data retention on Zoom and the third-party platform (Slack, WebEx, or MS Teams) will follow customers’ data configurations on the platforms.

What message metadata does Mio store and how long is it stored for?

Message Identifier (ID), timestamp, platform assigned user IDs and/or channel IDs, and associated identifiers. These are stored for the duration of the service contract, or until Mio is asked to destroy it via a hard delete (subject to Mio adhering to applicable state and federal laws).

Q: Does Mio store any Personal Identifiable Information (PII)?

Mio stores four PII values:

  • First name
  • Last name
  • Email address
  • Avatar/Profile Image

By default, a customer’s PII is stored on Mio for the duration of his or her contract. The data may be deleted within one month after the contract ends, at the latest, with the exception of data that is required to establish proof of a right or a contract, which will be stored for the duration provided by enforceable law.

Q: How does Mio work with regard to data loss prevention?

Mio currently employs ThreatStack to proactively monitor all of its production servers and infrastructure 24 hours a day. Agents are installed on every production instance and look for system anomalies and possible attempts of data exfiltration. A tested and proven escalation process is in place for Mio staff to react to any concerns that might be raised.

Data Encryption

Q: Does Mio Interoperability support Advanced Chat Encryption (ACE)?

Advanced Chat Encryption (ACE) is the End-to-End Encryption (E2EE) in Zoom Chat. It has to be disabled to enable Mio Interoperability.

E2EE *almost* never exists. When you add a bot or app to a conversation, EE2E has to be disabled because there is a third-party relay point. This is true regardless of what platform you’re on - Slack, MS Teams. In fact, Slack does not support E2EE. It is important to note that E2EE does not equal privacy or security. As long as you have turned on chat archiving, it breaks the principle of E2EE. And chat archiving is required at large organizations because FEC requires at least 5 years of archiving in case they have to subpoena chat records for events such as collusion. Arguably, the term E2E encryption is not an accurate representation, more like it is to describe the number of parties involved.

Q: What happens if customers turn on Advanced Chat Encryption (ACE) after integrating Mio?

Account admin has to disable “chatbot in channel” manually before enabling E2E. Once disabled, Mio (and other bots) will be removed from channels and interoperability will stop working.

Q: Does Mio encrypt all data?

When data is in transit between connected platforms, Mio will connect to the API using TLS 1.2 or later, typically over the HTTPS protocol. For data at rest, data will be encrypted with a minimum industry standard of AES-256 encryption. Mio classifies all customer data, and as a minimum, all our persistent storage has file storage encryption enabled. For higher classified data, we will perform additional encryption at the field level using an HSM-backed AWS KMS service.

Messages processed by Mio are never stored in an unencrypted format. Inbound events are immediately encrypted and only decrypted on demand when a transformative action is required. Translation typically occurs in milliseconds and in memory, greatly limiting exposure and potential attack vectors. Once translation and delivery are complete, the original and translated payloads are destroyed.

Server & Technology

Q: Where is Mio hosted?

Mio is currently hosted exclusively in AWS data centers in the U.S. They utilize multi-zone redundancy to maximize availability and uptime. All customer data is currently retained in the US.

Q: If AWS fails in one region, does Mio move over to another?

Mio utilizes multi-zone redundancy to maximize availability and uptime.

Q: Can customers choose which region to store their data in?

No, Mio reserved the right to failover to any AWS data center in the U.S. as part of their business continuity plan.

Q: Can Mio host its service outside of AWS, such as Oracle Cloud, and MS Azure?

Mio is working on making its service available on MS Azure. Oracle Cloud does not have many dependencies to support Mio needs, i.e. queuing system. Mio spent a good amount of Q1 assessing various cloud services and is redesigning its infrastructure to enable its service to run on other cloud platforms with Azure being our top priority. This is a significant project, and we foresee the implementation work taking up most of H2 of 2022.

Q: Does Mio support multi-cloud?

Mio does not support multi-cloud but it is on the roadmap and pending prioritization.

Q: Does Mio support Bring Your Own Key (BYOK)?

Mio does not support BYOK. Only one customer has requested it so far. We will assess based on demand.

Q: What happens to messages when Mio is down or when platform APIs are down?

Mio’s ability to deal with partner outages requires an inbound and outbound replay strategy. Partners such as Slack have an automatic redelivery mechanism where, should a Mio resource be unavailable, they will resend the event multiple times until successful or they will otherwise give up. Mio’s outbound reliability is defined by their own queue replay strategy. Should a target partner platform be unavailable, Mio will retain the encrypted event in a queue, and will automatically attempt redelivery based on a time-based replay strategy. In the case of Zoom, Teams, and Webex, Mio will disregard the message after five delivery attempts. Permanent failures are reported internally and monitored for further investigation and escalation where necessary.

Q: Are we going to be offering trial licenses?

We don’t offer typical trial licenses like other products but sales can request a 100% discount on Mio licenses for customers per their manager’s approval. We offer a 2-phased testing process: 1. POC in demo accounts, 2. Pilot in their environment.

  1. POC – We would start with a POC where we create 20 demo accounts for them in Mio-hosted Zoom, Teams, and Webex environments that we pre-configure for them. This lasts for 2 weeks. The POC works like a model home; we set up everything for the customers, all they have to do is log in and play with the product. This way they can get a feel without investing much. We highly recommend starting with a POC because the installation process of Mio can be cumbersome – customers have to make time to install Mio on the different platforms, syncing users, and channels. It can be a turn-off to the customers having to do so much early on in the sales process.
  2. Pilot – We would install Mio in their production sandbox environment to show them the setup, deployment, and administration process. They can have as many testers as they want. This lasts for 4 weeks.

Industries

Q: Is this product offered on Zoom for Government (ZfG)?

FedRAMP and government services require all information system components to reside within the authorization boundary. Mio is a third-party service that translates and forwards chat messages outside of Zoom. The essence of Mio violates the compliance boundary of Zoom for Government.

Q: Is Mio available to the healthcare industry? Is Mio HIPAA compliant?

Mio is available to the healthcare industry. Zoom has both a DPA and BAA in place with Mio to ensure personal data is legally protected. Moreover, Mio is more like the postal service, thus not subject to HIPAA regulations as a covered entity or business associate (i.e. they just deliver the mail, and never store or read it). For details, see Mio’s HIPAA White Paper.

Q: Is Mio available to the education sector (EDU)?

Mio can provide a lot of value for the education sector, primarily management and administrative functions in the delivery of higher education, keep the universities running. Student use case shouldn’t be a focus of Mio (Zoom doesn’t have any student use case right now).

Q: Is Mio available to financial services?

Currently Zoom Chat is not heavily used in financial services due to compliance risk. Large companies have policies and processes in place to assess risks. Customers need to make sure third-party services like Mio meet their internal and meeting regulatory requirements.

Limitations & Compatibility with Other Zoom Products

Q: What does Mio not do?

Mio does not make phone or video calls interoperable, only chat messages. A full list of features is provided later in this guide.

Q: Does this integration work for both internal and external contacts on Zoom Chat?

We only support internal contacts, as in coworkers at the same company but on different chat platforms. The implementation for external contacts are very different and we have very limited demand at the moment. We will increase the priority based on customer demand.

Q: Does Mio work with Persistent Meetings Chat?

Mio Interoperability is not supported in Persistent Meetings Chat. Meetings is an integral part of Persistent Meetings Chat. Video interoperability isn’t possible today because many platforms have proprietary protocols in video conferencing standards. Users on other platforms like Teams, and Slack can chat with Zoom users but they are not able to join the Zoom meetings.

Q: Can Zoom users share White Board with non-Zoom users on other platforms via Mio?

White Board does not render on other platforms. Users invited to a Zoom Board must have a Zoom account to access the shared Board. However, many customers we spoke to who are using Slack or Meetings also have Zoom Meetings accounts and would be able to access White Board when a link is shared with them.

Q: How does Mio work with Cloud Storage?

Zoom Cloud must be enabled for Mio to work. Mio Interoperability is not compatible with SharePoint or Box because some platforms create their own instance of the uploaded files making collaboration impossible.
